# Reading Comics at Night on iPhone: Dark Mode and Eye Comfort Tips

It's 11 PM, the lights are off, and you're one chapter from the end of the arc. Every time you swipe to the next page, the bright white gutters and panel borders hit like a spotlight. You squint, you dim the screen as low as it goes, but the harsh contrast of black ink on white panels still feels like staring into a flashlight.

This is the particular problem with reading comics on a backlit screen at night. Unlike novels — where night mode tints the background amber and text turns softer — comics are image files. The reader can't change the "color" of the ink or the white space between panels the same way. The solution needs a different approach.

[BiblioFuse for iOS](https://bibliofuse.com/en/) has several settings designed specifically for nighttime reading comfort. Here's how to configure them.

## Why Comics Are Harder on the Eyes at Night

Books and articles present text on a background that a reading app fully controls. Switch to night mode, and the background turns dark, the text turns light, and your eyes relax.

Comics are different. The actual page — the artwork — is a fixed image. A reader app can tint the entire image, but that shifts the colors you see in the art. It can invert the image, which turns black ink white and white paper black — useful for some grayscale manga, but it distorts full-color comics dramatically.

What BiblioFuse does instead:

- Dims the overall screen brightness within the app, going below what iOS allows system-wide
- Switches all the reader UI — toolbar, background, navigation — to true dark mode
- Offers a gentle sepia warm tone that reduces blue light without inverting the artwork
- Follows the iOS system appearance setting automatically at sunset

## Setting Up BiblioFuse for Night Reading

### Step 1: Enable Dark Mode

Open any comic in [BiblioFuse](https://bibliofuse.com/en/) and tap the center of the screen to reveal the reading controls. Tap the **Settings** icon (gear or sliders). Under **Appearance**, switch to **Dark**.

In dark mode, all the app UI — the toolbar, the black background behind pages, the navigation strip — switches to near-black. This eliminates the bright chrome surrounding the page, which is often the main source of eye strain when the comic itself uses significant white space.

### Step 2: Use the In-App Brightness Slider

iOS limits how dim the system brightness can go — there's a floor below which the control won't drop. BiblioFuse includes an independent brightness control that goes lower than the iOS floor, which is useful for reading in a completely dark room without overloading your adapted eyes.

In the reading settings panel, find **Brightness** and drag the slider to your preferred level. This setting persists across reading sessions, so you only configure it once.

For maximum comfort at night, combine low brightness with the sepia filter described in the next step.

### Step 3: Apply the Sepia or Warm Tone Filter

In reading settings, look for a **Sepia** or **Warm** filter option. This shifts the overall color temperature toward amber, reducing the cool blue light that suppresses melatonin and makes it harder to sleep after reading.

The sepia filter doesn't invert colors or distort the artwork — it adds a warm overlay, similar to having a warm-tinted screen protector. Full-color comics retain their color relationships; they simply look warmer overall. For grayscale manga, the sepia filter adds a classic ink-on-paper look that many readers prefer even during the day.

### Step 4: Use iOS Night Shift and True Tone

These system-level features complement BiblioFuse's own controls:

**Night Shift** (Settings → Display & Brightness → Night Shift): shifts the entire display toward warmer colors after sunset, reducing blue light across all apps. Enable it on a schedule or set it to automatic.

**True Tone** (Settings → Display & Brightness → True Tone): on supported iPhones and iPads, True Tone continuously adjusts the white balance to match ambient room lighting, making whites look less harsh under warm light sources.

Use these alongside BiblioFuse's dark mode and brightness settings for the most comfortable combination.

### Step 5: Adjust Auto-Lock for Uninterrupted Reading

Nothing breaks reading immersion like the screen going dark mid-chapter. Go to **Settings → Display & Brightness → Auto-Lock** and set it to **5 minutes** or **Never** while reading, then restore it to a shorter value afterward.

BiblioFuse respects the system setting and won't lock the screen while a page is being displayed.

## Manga at Night: Special Considerations

Manga pages are typically black and white with high contrast, making them especially sensitive to bright rendering in a dark room. A single white panel on a screen at full brightness can feel blinding when your eyes are fully dark-adapted.

For manga in BiblioFuse at night:

- **Dark mode + low brightness** as the baseline
- **Sepia filter** is particularly effective on grayscale pages — it softens stark white areas into a warm cream tone
- For older scans with slightly grey or off-white backgrounds, the sepia filter ties everything into a cohesive warm palette

## OLED and ProMotion: Why Hardware Matters

On iPhones with OLED displays (iPhone X and later), true black — `#000000` — uses zero power and emits no light. BiblioFuse's dark mode uses near-black backgrounds that approach this ideal, meaning the chrome around comic pages is essentially invisible in a dark room and won't add to the overall brightness.

On ProMotion iPhones (iPhone 13 Pro and later), the display can step down to 1 Hz when showing static content — in practice, a still comic page. This reduces eye strain from refresh flickering and cuts battery consumption during long reading sessions.

## Automatic Night Settings: Set It and Forget It

BiblioFuse follows the iOS system appearance. If you have iOS configured to auto-switch between light and dark mode at sunset, BiblioFuse matches it automatically — no manual toggle needed when the room gets dark.

Enable this at **Settings → Display & Brightness → Automatic** and configure your preferred light/dark schedule. Pair it with Night Shift on the same schedule for a seamless transition to night-friendly reading as the sun goes down.
