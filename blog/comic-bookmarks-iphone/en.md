BiblioFuse lets you drop bookmarks anywhere inside a comic or ebook on iPhone — not just at chapter breaks, but at any page you choose. Bookmarks sync across iPhone, iPad, and Mac via iCloud, so your saved spots follow you regardless of which device you pick up next.

## The Problem: Picking Up Where You Left Off

You're 80 pages into a collected volume of Saga. Your phone dies. Later you open the app on your iPad and the volume opens at the beginning. You swipe forward trying to remember where you were — was it page 78? 82? Was the scene with the Will character or the flashback? A bookmark made on your iPhone the moment before the battery died would have dropped you exactly back where you left off, on any device, instantly.

This scenario plays out constantly for heavy readers who switch between devices, share an app with family members, or re-read long series years later. Without a reliable bookmark system, location in a comic becomes something you have to track mentally or with external notes. That friction compounds across dozens of volumes.

## How to Add a Bookmark in BiblioFuse

### Step 1: Open a Comic in the Reader

Launch BiblioFuse and open any comic, manga, or ebook. Navigate to the page you want to bookmark — whether it's the last page you read, a great splash page, or the start of a new chapter.

### Step 2: Tap the Screen to Reveal Controls

Tap once in the center of the reading area. The reading controls fade in: a navigation bar at the top and a page slider at the bottom. Look for the **Bookmark** icon (a ribbon or flag icon) in the top navigation bar.

### Step 3: Tap the Bookmark Icon

Tap the bookmark icon. BiblioFuse saves a bookmark for the current page. The icon fills to indicate a bookmark is active on this page. You can tap it again to remove the bookmark if you placed it by mistake.

### Step 4: Access Your Bookmarks

Tap the **Contents** or **Bookmark List** button (typically a list icon near the bookmark icon) to open your list of saved bookmarks for this volume. Each entry shows the page number and the date you saved it. Tap any bookmark to jump directly to that page.

### Step 5: Navigate Between Bookmarks

Within the bookmark list, tap any entry to return to that exact page. You can also delete bookmarks by swiping left on a list entry. Your bookmark list is visible from any device signed into the same Apple ID.

## Using Bookmarks for Different Reading Styles

**Single reading session:** Drop a bookmark when you stop reading. The next time you open the volume, your saved position is the first entry in the bookmark list — one tap and you're back.

**Tracking story arcs:** Long manga series often have multiple story arcs within a single collected volume. Bookmark the first page of each arc you want to revisit. The bookmark list becomes a table of contents you built yourself.

**Sharing with a family member:** If you share an iPhone with a partner or child, each person can bookmark their own position in shared volumes. Since bookmarks are tied to the iCloud account, each person's reading progress is tracked separately on their own device.

**Marking favorite panels or spreads:** Bookmarks aren't only for tracking progress. Use them to save standout double-page spreads, emotional beats, or panels you want to return to. Your bookmark list doubles as a curated reading highlight reel for the volume.

## Bookmarks vs. Reading Progress: What's the Difference?

BiblioFuse tracks two kinds of position data. **Reading progress** is automatic — the app records the last page you visited each time you close a volume. This is how the app knows to resume from page 80 even if you never dropped a bookmark.

**Bookmarks** are intentional — you place them manually to mark pages you want to return to for a reason beyond just progress tracking. Think of reading progress as your cursor in a document, and bookmarks as the highlighter you use to flag passages you'll reference again.

Both sync via iCloud. Both are available on any device you own. Together they ensure you never lose your place and never lose the pages that matter to you most.

## iCloud Sync and What It Means in Practice

Bookmarks sync through iCloud Drive using BiblioFuse's private container — they don't require a BiblioFuse account or any external login. As long as iCloud Drive is enabled on your iPhone, iPad, and Mac, your bookmarks propagate within seconds of being placed.

This works even when you're offline. BiblioFuse writes your bookmarks locally and syncs them to iCloud when your device reconnects. If you're reading on a plane without Wi-Fi, your bookmarks are saved. When you land and reconnect, they sync to all your other devices automatically.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do bookmarks sync when I'm offline?**
Yes. BiblioFuse saves bookmarks locally and syncs them to iCloud once your device connects to the internet. You won't lose any bookmarks placed while offline.

**How many bookmarks can I add to a single volume?**
There is no practical limit. You can bookmark as many pages as you need within a volume. The bookmark list is scrollable, so even volumes with dozens of saved points remain navigable.

**Will my bookmarks survive if I delete and reinstall BiblioFuse?**
Yes, as long as your bookmarks have synced to iCloud before you uninstall the app. After reinstalling, BiblioFuse downloads your bookmark data from iCloud automatically.

**Can I bookmark specific panels or just pages?**
BiblioFuse bookmarks save at the page level. If you're reading in a strip-scroll layout, the bookmark records the page position in that layout. Panel-level precision isn't currently supported, but page-level bookmarks cover the vast majority of use cases.

**Do bookmarks work in EPUB files as well as CBZ?**
Yes. Bookmarks work across all formats BiblioFuse supports — CBZ, CBR, EPUB, PDF, ZIP, RAR, and TXT. The same tap-to-bookmark flow applies in every format.
