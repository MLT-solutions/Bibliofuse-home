How to Sideload Comics to iPhone Without iTunes or iCloud

June 9, 2026

How to Sideload Comics to iPhone Without iTunes or iCloud

BiblioFuse reads CBZ, CBR, EPUB, PDF, ZIP, RAR, and TXT files on iPhone and iPad running iOS 17 or later, and includes a built-in Wi-Fi Transfer feature so you can copy comics directly from any Mac or PC browser — no iTunes, no cable, and no iCloud account needed. Open the Wi-Fi Transfer page from inside BiblioFuse, then drag and drop files in your browser and they appear in your library in seconds.

Why Sideloading Comics to iPhone Without iTunes Is Now the Standard Approach

Apple removed the dedicated iTunes desktop app from macOS Catalina onwards. File sharing for iOS apps moved to Finder's device panel — you connect a cable, select your device, click File Sharing, choose the app, and drag files into a small window. One cable, multiple steps, no drag-from-Downloads shortcut.

On Windows, iTunes still exists but it's heavy, often outdated, and requires a USB-C or Lightning cable. For laptop users who don't carry an adapter, or anyone who simply prefers wireless, cable-based transfer is an unnecessary obstacle.

And iCloud Drive, while convenient for individual files, requires an Apple ID, is limited by upload speed for bulk transfers, and still doesn't automate the handoff to a specific app on your phone.

Why Wi-Fi Transfer Is the Best Way to Sideload Comics to iPhone

BiblioFuse's built-in Wi-Fi Transfer requires no cable, no iTunes, no iCloud account, and no software installed on your Mac or PC. It runs a local web server inside the app and displays an IP address you open in any browser — Safari, Chrome, Firefox, Edge, it doesn't matter.

You drag and drop CBZ, CBR, EPUB, PDF, ZIP, RAR, or TXT files onto the browser page and they transfer at local network speed. On a typical home Wi-Fi connection, a 500 MB CBZ volume transfers in under 15 seconds. You can send entire series at once.

AirDrop is an alternative, but it's limited to Apple devices on the same local network and can be unreliable with large batches. Wi-Fi Transfer works from any device with a browser.

How to Sideload Comics to iPhone Without iTunes Using BiblioFuse

  1. Open BiblioFuse on your iPhone or iPad.
  2. Tap the Transfer tab at the bottom of the screen.
  3. BiblioFuse displays a local IP address and port — for example, http://192.168.1.42:8080.
  4. Open a browser on your Mac or PC and enter that address in the address bar.
  5. The BiblioFuse Wi-Fi Transfer page appears. Drag and drop your CBZ, EPUB, PDF, or other comic files directly onto the page.
  6. Files upload instantly and appear in your BiblioFuse library on iPhone.

Your iPhone and computer must be on the same Wi-Fi network. There is no per-batch file limit — transfer an entire library if needed.

Other Ways to Transfer Comics to iPhone Without a Cable

Method 2: Files App with iCloud Drive or Third-Party Cloud Storage

If your comics are already stored in iCloud Drive, Dropbox, Google Drive, or OneDrive:

  1. Open the Files app on iPhone.
  2. Navigate to your comic files in your cloud storage.
  3. Tap and hold a file, then choose Open in… and select BiblioFuse.

This works well for individual files or small batches when you're not on your home network. For bulk transfers, Wi-Fi Transfer is significantly faster.

Method 3: AirDrop (Mac to iPhone Only)

  1. Select one or more CBZ or EPUB files in Finder on your Mac.
  2. Right-click and choose Share → AirDrop.
  3. Select your iPhone from the AirDrop list.
  4. Accept the transfer on iPhone when prompted. Choose BiblioFuse to open the files.

AirDrop requires no app on the Mac side and is fast for moderate file counts. It uses Bluetooth-assisted direct Wi-Fi, so it works even without a router — but it only works between Apple devices.

Method 4: Safari Download and Open In

If you're downloading a comic file directly to iPhone from Safari:

  1. Tap the download link in Safari.
  2. Once downloaded, tap the file in the Downloads indicator (top-right).
  3. Tap and hold the file, then choose Share → Open in BiblioFuse.

This works for individual files and requires no computer at all. It's the fastest path when you're downloading directly on your iPhone.

Tips for Smooth Transfers

Keep BiblioFuse open and in the foreground during Wi-Fi Transfer. The local web server runs only while the app is active. Switching to another app pauses the transfer.

Use the same Wi-Fi network. Both devices must be on the same network. Guest networks and client-isolation settings on routers will block local connections. If the IP address doesn't load, temporarily disable any VPN.

Organise series into folders before transferring. Transfer one series folder at a time to keep your iPhone library organised. If you dump 200 files at once, sorting them afterwards takes longer than organising up front.

Short filenames transfer more reliably. Avoid colons, slashes, and special characters in filenames, especially on Windows. Rename problem files before dragging them to the browser.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I sideload comics to iPhone without a computer? Yes. Open the Files app on iPhone, navigate to comics stored in iCloud Drive, Dropbox, or any Files-compatible provider, and tap Open in BiblioFuse. You can also download comics directly in Safari and open them in BiblioFuse via the Share sheet.

Does BiblioFuse Wi-Fi Transfer work with all comic formats? Yes. Wi-Fi Transfer accepts CBZ, CBR, EPUB, PDF, ZIP, RAR, and TXT files — every format BiblioFuse supports. Drag and drop any combination of formats in a single session.

Do I need an account to use BiblioFuse Wi-Fi Transfer? No. Wi-Fi Transfer is a local network feature built into the app. No account, no subscription, and no internet connection are required — just a shared Wi-Fi network between your iPhone and the device running the browser.

Why doesn't the BiblioFuse Wi-Fi Transfer address load in my browser? Both devices must be on the same Wi-Fi network (not one on Ethernet and another on a guest network). If you have a VPN active on either device, disable it temporarily. Some routers enable client isolation by default — check your router settings if local connections fail.

Is there a file size limit for Wi-Fi Transfer in BiblioFuse? No. BiblioFuse Wi-Fi Transfer has no per-file or total-session size limit. You can transfer a 2 GB CBZ archive or an entire library folder without interruption.

What replaced iTunes file sharing for transferring files to iPhone apps? Apple moved iOS app file sharing to Finder on Mac (in the device panel under File Sharing) when it removed the standalone iTunes app in 2019. On Windows, the legacy iTunes app still handles this. BiblioFuse's Wi-Fi Transfer replaces both — no cable or iTunes needed.
