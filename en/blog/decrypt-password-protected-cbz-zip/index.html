How to Open Password-Protected CBZ and ZIP Files on iPhone

SmartDecrypt PDF ZIP CBZ opens password-protected CBZ and ZIP archives directly on iPhone, iPad, and Mac without uploading your files to any server. The app decrypts CBZ comic archives and ZIP files on-device using a Rust-based engine, then saves the unlocked copy to your Files app. After decryption, open your CBZ comics in BiblioFuse for the best reading experience on iPhone. Download SmartDecrypt PDF ZIP CBZ from the App Store to remove archive passwords in seconds.

Why Password-Protected CBZ Files Won't Open in Most Comic Readers

You download a CBZ comic archive from a publisher, a fan translation group, or your own backup system. You try to open it in your comic reader, but instead of page one, you get a password prompt — or nothing at all. Most iPhone comic readers, including BiblioFuse, can open standard CBZ and CBR files without any issue, but they are not designed to decrypt password-protected archives. Decryption is a separate operation, and it needs to happen before the file reaches your reader.

This is where SmartDecrypt comes in. It sits upstream of your comic reader: you decrypt the archive first, then open the unlocked file in BiblioFuse or any other app.

Why You Shouldn't Use Online ZIP Decryptors

A quick web search turns up dozens of services that claim to unlock ZIP and CBZ archives online. The workflow looks convenient — upload, enter password, download unlocked file — but the problem is step one: upload.

Comic archives often contain licensed content. ZIP files may contain personal documents, photos, or backups. Uploading either to a third-party server means your content leaves your device and lands on infrastructure you have no control over. Free services often retain files. Privacy policies rarely cover what happens to content after processing.

SmartDecrypt runs entirely on your device. No upload, no network request, no third-party access to your files.

How to Open a Password-Protected CBZ or ZIP File on iPhone

  1. Install SmartDecrypt PDF ZIP CBZ from the App Store on your iPhone or iPad.
  2. Locate the file. Find the password-protected CBZ or ZIP in the Files app, in an email attachment, or in a Safari download.
  3. Share to SmartDecrypt. Tap Share on the file, then choose SmartDecrypt from the share sheet. The file loads instantly into the app. Alternatively, open SmartDecrypt directly and import the file from the Files browser.
  4. Enter the password. Type the password for the archive. If you've saved a password profile for this publisher or collection, select it from the profile list instead.
  5. Tap Decrypt. SmartDecrypt processes the archive on-device and saves the unlocked copy to your Files app. The original protected file remains unchanged.
  6. Open in your reader. Tap the decrypted file and choose BiblioFuse to read it. The archive opens normally — no password prompt.

Practical Scenarios

Fan Translation Groups

Many fan translation CBZ archives are distributed with password protection, often with the password posted on a website or Discord channel alongside the download link. SmartDecrypt makes this seamless: decrypt in seconds, then open in BiblioFuse for reading.

Publisher Digital Downloads

Some comic publishers and digital manga platforms distribute archives with password protection as a lightweight DRM measure. Once you've purchased and downloaded the file, SmartDecrypt lets you remove the restriction so the file works with any app on any of your devices.

ZIP Archives with Personal Content

ZIP files aren't only for comics — they're used for photo backups, document archives, and file collections of all kinds. SmartDecrypt handles standard ZIP files with the same workflow: share, decrypt, access the contents.

Does the Share Extension Work Across All Apps?

Yes. The SmartDecrypt Share Extension appears in the iOS share sheet for any file type the app supports. This means you can trigger SmartDecrypt directly from Mail, Files, Safari, or any third-party app that supports file sharing. You never need to leave the app you're in — the decryption dialog slides up from the share sheet.

What Happens to the Original File?

SmartDecrypt never modifies the original file. After decryption, you have two files: the original password-protected archive and a new, unlocked copy. The unlocked copy is saved in your Files app in a SmartDecrypt output folder. You can then move the unlocked file anywhere — to iCloud Drive, to another app, or directly into BiblioFuse.
