How to Organize Manga by Series on iPhone with BiblioFuse

BiblioFuse lets you organize manga by series on iPhone using its built-in tag system — tag each volume with the series name, then filter your library to see every volume in reading order. The app runs on iOS 17 or later and supports CBZ, CBR, EPUB, PDF, ZIP, RAR, and TXT files. Tags and ratings sync across iPhone, iPad, and Mac via iCloud.

The Problem: 50 Volumes, Zero Order

You've built an impressive manga collection — One Piece, Naruto, Attack on Titan, Berserk — all sitting in one flat library. When you finish volume 23 and want to jump straight to volume 24, you're scrolling through a wall of covers trying to remember which one comes next. Worse, volumes from different series are interspersed, so finding a specific title feels like hunting through a pile of unorganized books. This is the frustration that a good organization system solves.

Why Folders Alone Aren't Enough

BiblioFuse is not a file browser. It's a full reading library with metadata, smart filtering, and iCloud-backed sync — which means the way you organize your manga goes far beyond which folder a file lives in. A folder structure on your Mac might look tidy, but once files land in BiblioFuse, what matters is the metadata you attach to each volume. Tags turn your flat library into a searchable, filterable collection organized exactly the way you read.

When you tag volumes with a series name, you create a persistent, device-agnostic view of your collection. You don't need to re-sort anything when you switch from iPhone to iPad. You don't need to remember folder paths. You just tap the series tag and your volumes appear in order.

How to Organize Manga by Series in BiblioFuse on iPhone

Follow these steps to set up a clean, series-based organization system for your manga library.

Step 1: Open BiblioFuse and Go to Your Library

Launch BiblioFuse on your iPhone. Tap the Library tab at the bottom of the screen. You'll see your full collection of manga volumes displayed as cover art.

Step 2: Tag a Volume with Its Series Name

Long-press any manga volume to bring up the context menu. Tap Tag, then type the series name — for example, "One Piece". Confirm the tag. The volume is now associated with that series label, and this association will sync to your other devices automatically via iCloud.

Step 3: Tag All Remaining Volumes in the Series

Repeat the tagging process for every volume in the series. For large collections, use batch select: tap Select in the top-right corner, choose all volumes from the same series, then apply the tag to all of them at once. This saves significant time when you're organizing a 100-volume run.

Step 4: Filter Your Library by Series Tag

Once all volumes are tagged, tap the Tags filter icon in your library. Select "One Piece" from the list. BiblioFuse now shows only the volumes carrying that tag — your entire One Piece run, isolated from everything else.

Step 5: Set Up a Consistent Reading Order with Filenames

BiblioFuse sorts volumes alphabetically by filename within a tag view. To ensure your volumes appear in the correct reading order, name your files consistently before adding them to the library. Use a format like:

  • One Piece v01.cbz
  • One Piece v02.cbz
  • One Piece v03.cbz

This zero-padded numbering (v01, v02, v03 rather than v1, v2, v3) prevents v10 from appearing before v2 in alphabetical sort. Consistent naming is the single most effective way to guarantee correct reading order across all your series.

Step 6: Use Ratings to Track Your Reading Progress

BiblioFuse supports 1–5 star ratings on every volume. Use ratings to mark your reading progress — for example, 5 stars for volumes you've finished and want to remember fondly, 3 stars for volumes you're currently reading, and 1 star for volumes still in your backlog. You can filter by rating to quickly find what to read next.

Practical Examples: Tagging, Wi-Fi Transfer, and iCloud Sync

Fully tagging a series: If you have all 107 volumes of One Piece, tag them all with "One Piece" using batch select. Once tagged, filtering by the "One Piece" tag gives you the complete run, and alphabetical filename sorting puts them in volume order automatically.

Using Wi-Fi Transfer to add volumes by series: On your Mac, organize your manga into folders by series — one folder named "One Piece", another named "Berserk". Open BiblioFuse's Wi-Fi Transfer feature (found in Settings), then drag volumes from each folder into the browser upload page. You can add an entire series folder in one drag operation, then tag all those volumes at once in BiblioFuse.

iCloud sync keeping everything consistent: Any tag or rating you apply on your iPhone syncs to your iPad and Mac automatically through iCloud. If you tag 10 volumes on your iPhone while commuting, those tags are waiting for you when you sit down at your iPad later. You never have to organize the same collection twice.

How Does BiblioFuse Handle Series Organization Differently?

Managing manga purely through filenames and folders works on a desktop, but breaks down on a mobile reading app. BiblioFuse's tag system gives you the flexibility of metadata without the rigidity of a fixed folder structure. You can tag one volume with multiple tags — "One Piece" and "Completed" and "Favorites" — and it will appear in all three filtered views simultaneously. That's something no folder system can replicate.

Search also works across filenames and metadata, so even if you forget which tag you used, typing "One Piece" in the search bar surfaces all matching volumes instantly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can BiblioFuse automatically detect and group manga series? BiblioFuse does not auto-detect series from CBZ metadata at this time. You assign series tags manually, which gives you full control over how volumes are grouped — including custom groupings that metadata alone wouldn't support.

Do tags sync to my iPad automatically? Yes. Tags and ratings sync across all your devices — iPhone, iPad, and Mac — via iCloud. As long as iCloud Drive is enabled and you're signed into the same Apple ID, your organization is consistent everywhere.

How many tags can I add to a single volume? You can add multiple tags to any volume. There is no fixed limit. This lets you organize the same volume under several categories simultaneously — for example, "One Piece", "Shonen", and "Currently Reading".

Does BiblioFuse read series metadata embedded in CBZ or EPUB files? BiblioFuse reads file content for display purposes. For organization, the tag system is the primary method. Manual tagging ensures your groupings are always accurate regardless of how the source file was packaged.

Can I sort volumes in a custom reading order within a series tag? Within a tag view, volumes sort alphabetically by filename. The most reliable way to control reading order is consistent, zero-padded filename numbering (v01, v02, v03). This guarantees the correct order regardless of when volumes were added.

What file formats does BiblioFuse support for manga? BiblioFuse supports CBZ, CBR, ZIP, RAR, EPUB, PDF, and TXT files. CBZ and CBR are the most common manga archive formats and are fully supported on iOS 17 and later.
