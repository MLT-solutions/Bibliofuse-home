# How to Use AirDrop to Transfer Comics and Ebooks to iPhone

BiblioFuse accepts files shared via AirDrop on iPhone and iPad running iOS 17 or later. When someone shares a CBZ, CBR, EPUB, PDF, ZIP, RAR, or TXT file with you via AirDrop, you can open it directly in BiblioFuse from the share sheet. The file is saved to your BiblioFuse library and syncs to your other Apple devices via iCloud automatically.

## Why AirDrop Is One of the Fastest Ways to Get Comics onto iPhone

AirDrop transfers files at local Wi-Fi or Bluetooth speeds with no internet connection and no file size limit. A 400 MB CBZ comic can land on your iPhone in under ten seconds when you are near the sending Mac or iPad. There is no account to log in to, no cloud service to route through, and the transfer is encrypted end-to-end.

For comic and manga readers, AirDrop is especially useful when sharing files between your own devices. If you download a new manga volume on your Mac and want to read it on your iPhone right away, AirDrop sends it directly — no USB cable, no email attachment with a 25 MB limit, and no waiting for iCloud Drive to sync a large file. [BiblioFuse](/en/) receives the file and adds it to your library instantly.

## How to AirDrop a Comic or Ebook to iPhone

The process works from any Apple device — Mac, iPad, or another iPhone — to your iPhone running BiblioFuse.

### From a Mac

1. Open **Finder** on your Mac and locate the comic file (CBZ, EPUB, PDF, etc.).
2. Right-click the file and choose **Share → AirDrop**.
3. Select your iPhone from the list of nearby AirDrop recipients.
4. On your iPhone, a notification appears asking you to accept the file. Tap **Accept**.
5. iOS asks which app should open the file. Tap **BiblioFuse**.
6. The file is imported into BiblioFuse and appears in your library.

### From an iPad or another iPhone

1. Open the **Files** app and find the comic file.
2. Long-press the file and tap **Share**.
3. Tap the **AirDrop** icon and choose your iPhone.
4. Accept the file on your iPhone and open it with BiblioFuse.

### Sharing directly from a comic reader app

If you are on a Mac using another reader and want to send a file to your iPhone, you can usually drag the file from that app to the Desktop, then AirDrop it from Finder. Alternatively, use **Share → AirDrop** from any app that supports the macOS Share menu.

## Make Sure AirDrop Is On Before You Start

AirDrop requires both Wi-Fi and Bluetooth to be enabled on both devices. Neither device needs to be connected to the same network — AirDrop creates a direct peer-to-peer connection. Keep both devices within about 9 metres (30 feet) of each other for reliable transfers.

**On iPhone:** Open Control Centre and long-press the network tile (top-left cluster). Tap **AirDrop** and choose **Everyone for 10 Minutes** or **Contacts Only** depending on your preference. If you are receiving from your own Mac, **Contacts Only** is safest.

**On Mac:** Open Finder, click **AirDrop** in the sidebar, and set **Allow me to be discovered by** to **Everyone** or **Contacts Only** at the bottom of the window.

## What Happens After the File Arrives in BiblioFuse

When you accept an AirDropped file and choose BiblioFuse as the destination, BiblioFuse copies the file into its internal library folder. From that point:

- The file appears in your library under the relevant section (comics, ebooks, or all files).
- iCloud sync sends the file to your other BiblioFuse-enabled Apple devices automatically if iCloud is enabled.
- BiblioFuse reads the file's format (CBZ, EPUB, PDF, etc.) and opens it with the correct reader engine.
- Any reading progress, bookmarks, or tags you add sync across devices via iCloud.

You do not need to manually place the file in a folder or rename it. BiblioFuse handles the import automatically.

## AirDrop vs Wi-Fi Transfer: Which Should You Use?

BiblioFuse also has a built-in **Wi-Fi Transfer** feature that lets you drag files from any browser on a Mac or PC — including Windows — without needing AirDrop. Here is when to use each:

| Situation | Best Method |
|-----------|-------------|
| Sending from a Mac, iPad, or iPhone | AirDrop |
| Sending from a Windows PC | Wi-Fi Transfer |
| Sending from a Linux machine | Wi-Fi Transfer |
| Large batch of files at once | Wi-Fi Transfer (drag multiple) |
| Quick single file between your own Apple devices | AirDrop |
| Sharing from a friend's device | AirDrop |

For Windows users, open BiblioFuse on your iPhone, tap the **Wi-Fi Transfer** button, then open the displayed address in any browser on your PC. You can drag and drop multiple files at once without any app installed on the PC side.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I AirDrop CBZ files directly to BiblioFuse on iPhone?

Yes. When you AirDrop a CBZ file to your iPhone, iOS will show a share sheet asking which app to open it with. Choose BiblioFuse and the file is imported into your library immediately. BiblioFuse supports CBZ, CBR, EPUB, PDF, ZIP, RAR, and TXT files on iOS 17 or later.

### Does AirDrop work for large comic files?

Yes. AirDrop has no file size limit and transfers at local Wi-Fi speeds, making it well-suited for large CBZ or EPUB files. A 300–500 MB comic file typically transfers in under 30 seconds when the devices are close together.

### Why is my iPhone not showing up as an AirDrop recipient?

The most common causes are: AirDrop is set to **Receiving Off** on the iPhone, Bluetooth is disabled on either device, the devices are too far apart, or the iPhone screen is locked. Turn on AirDrop in Control Centre on your iPhone and keep both devices close together with screens unlocked.

### Can I AirDrop an EPUB ebook to BiblioFuse instead of Apple Books?

Yes. When iOS shows the app selection after you accept the AirDrop file, tap **BiblioFuse**. If the share sheet defaults to Apple Books, tap **More** or **Open in Another App** to find BiblioFuse. You can also set BiblioFuse as the default handler for EPUB by opening any EPUB via the Files app and choosing BiblioFuse from the open-in menu.

### What if I want to send comics to iPhone from a Windows PC?

AirDrop only works between Apple devices, so you cannot use it from Windows. Instead, use BiblioFuse's built-in **Wi-Fi Transfer** feature: tap the transfer icon in BiblioFuse on your iPhone, then open the displayed URL in a browser on your Windows PC and drag files directly to the page.

### Does the AirDropped file stay on my iPhone if I delete it from my Mac?

Yes. Once BiblioFuse receives and imports the file, it stores its own copy in the app's library folder. Deleting the original from your Mac does not affect the copy inside BiblioFuse on your iPhone.

## Start Reading Your Comics Without Wires

AirDrop is the fastest way to move comics and ebooks from one Apple device to another. Once BiblioFuse has the file, you can pick up reading immediately — no sync delay, no account login, no steps in between.

Download [BiblioFuse](/en/) from the App Store to start building your wireless reading library. For transfers from Windows or for larger batches, the built-in Wi-Fi Transfer feature is always there as an alternative.
