# Using Bookmarks in BiblioFuse: Never Lose Your Place in a Comic

BiblioFuse bookmarks let you save any page in a comic, manga, or EPUB with a custom name so you can jump back to it instantly. The feature works on iPhone and iPad running iOS 17 or later, and every bookmark syncs automatically across all your devices via iCloud. Whether you are mid-chapter or mid-series, your place is always saved.

## The Problem Every Comic Reader Knows

You finished a late-night reading session halfway through volume three of a long manga series. You set your iPhone down, life got busy, and a week later you pick it back up. You swipe through pages trying to remember where you stopped — was it chapter twelve or thirteen? You check the reading app, but its last-read indicator jumped because you accidentally opened the wrong volume.

This is the moment that BiblioFuse bookmarks were built for.

## Why Page Number Memory Doesn't Work

Telling yourself to "just remember page 247" stops working the moment you have more than two or three series on the go. Different apps count pages differently. Some series have multiple volumes that restart at page one. And if you switch between your iPhone and your iPad, the apps that track only a single last-read position can fall out of sync.

Bookmarks solve all of this because they are named, persistent, and tied to a specific page rather than a general reading position.

## How to Use Comic Bookmarks in BiblioFuse on iPhone

BiblioFuse makes bookmarking a two-tap operation. Here is how it works from start to finish.

### Step-by-Step Bookmarking

1. Open any comic, manga, EPUB, or PDF in [BiblioFuse](/en/).
2. Navigate to the page you want to save.
3. Tap the bookmark icon (a ribbon or flag icon) in the reading toolbar — it usually appears when you tap the center of the screen to reveal controls.
4. A panel slides up asking you to name your bookmark. Type something descriptive, like "Start of arc 2" or "Favourite panel — chapter 7."
5. Tap Save. The bookmark is created immediately.
6. To return to it later, tap the Bookmarks icon from the book's detail screen or from within the reader. Your full list appears.
7. Tap any bookmark in the list to jump directly to that page.

You can add unlimited bookmarks per book, so there is no need to be selective. Bookmark every chapter start, every key panel, every page you want to revisit.

### Bookmark a Favourite Panel

Bookmarks are not only for tracking progress. If a splash page or a character reveal catches your eye, bookmark it. Give it a name like "Best fight scene vol 4" and you can return to it any time — even after you have read hundreds of pages past it.

### Organize Chapter Starts

For long series, bookmark the first page of every chapter with a name that includes the chapter number. This turns your bookmark list into a personal table of contents that works across every format BiblioFuse supports, including CBZ, CBR, ZIP, RAR, EPUB, PDF, and TXT.

## Why iCloud Sync Makes Bookmarks Even More Useful

Every bookmark you create on your iPhone appears automatically on your iPad and Mac without any extra steps. This is powered by iCloud sync, which is built into [BiblioFuse](/en/) and requires no account beyond your existing Apple ID.

You can start reading on the couch on your iPhone, add a bookmark, then pick up your iPad in the bedroom and jump straight to the same page. The sync happens in the background, so the bookmark is there when you need it.

## Practical Scenarios: Getting the Most from Comic Bookmarks on iPhone

### Tracking Multiple Series at Once

If you are following five different manga series, bookmarks give each series its own named save point. Instead of relying on one "last read" marker per book, you can have multiple bookmarks in a single volume — one for your current reading position and others for panels you plan to share or re-read.

### Bookmarking Key Panels for Reference

Artists and writers often revisit older volumes to check continuity or study panel composition. With BiblioFuse, you can build a personal reference library inside each book. Bookmark the panel where a character's design changes, or the page where a plot thread is first introduced, and retrieve it in seconds rather than scrolling through hundreds of pages.

### Sharing Page References with Friends

When you want to tell a friend "you have to see this panel," a bookmark with the exact page number lets you share the reference precisely. You can note the bookmark name in a message and your friend can navigate to the same page in their own copy.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I bookmark a page in BiblioFuse?**
While reading, tap the center of the screen to reveal the reading toolbar, then tap the bookmark icon. A dialog appears where you can type a name for the bookmark. Tap Save and the bookmark is added to your list for that book.

**Do BiblioFuse bookmarks sync to iPad?**
Yes. Bookmarks sync automatically via iCloud to every device signed into the same Apple ID, including iPhone, iPad, and Mac. There is no manual sync step required.

**Can I name bookmarks in BiblioFuse?**
Yes. Every time you create a bookmark, BiblioFuse prompts you to enter a custom name. You can write anything — a chapter reference, a short description of the scene, or a personal note.

**How many bookmarks can I add per book?**
There is no limit. You can add as many bookmarks as you like to a single book. This makes it practical to bookmark every chapter start, every favourite panel, and every page you plan to revisit.

**Do bookmarks work for EPUB files too?**
Yes. BiblioFuse bookmarks work across every format the app supports: EPUB, PDF, CBZ, CBR, ZIP, RAR, and TXT. The bookmark experience is the same regardless of file format.
